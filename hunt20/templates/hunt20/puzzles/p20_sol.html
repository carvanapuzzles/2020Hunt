First, solve the logic puzzle. Here's a screenshot of the final grid with letters as points of interest and Xs as fountains. The 1s trace the path to A, the 2s to B, etc. Fountains are colored in by the route they are on (we'll get to the street and avenue names in a second).

The letters spell OWL CITY. Not only can we see why this puzzle might make heads spin, the clues for the streets and avenues make more sense. Every street is named after an Owl City song and every avenue is named after a resident--a fictional owl (all of which are listed on Wikipedia's List of fictional birds of prey).

As mentioned in the instructions of the logic puzzle, each point of interest can be descibed as being at the "intersection" of a street and avenue. The street and avenue at the intersection each point of interest is at have the same number of letters in their name with exactly one letter overlapping. Extract this letter and read in A-F point of interest order.

The letters spell GESTALT, "an organized whole that is perceived as more than the sum of its parts" as well as a fitting answer.

Author's Notes
The last two Carvana Puzzle Hunts featured logic puzzles (Mental Block from 2017 and Going in Circles from 2019) that were fun and engaging to solve, but then unfortunately caused most solvers quite a lot of grief on the extraction. I joked at the start of writing we should just lean into this and explicitly call a puzzle "This Year's Logic Puzzle with a Difficult Extraction". Turns out, uh, we wrote a lot of those puzzles this year, just not in logic puzzle form. But this one is here too.
As mentioned in a few other notes, we like ending rounds with a big hairy puzzle. We call these "gatekeepers" (see Stranger Things from last year) while we're writing. It's always fun to see one get cracked--we were really impressed Puzzlers Anonymous got it with only one hint.
OK as far as the puzzle itself goes--it's a decently challenging logic puzzle, but definitely doable and very fun. I really like having the route to the Gymnasium be extremely vague and yet constrained by the rules. The idea for Owl City came as we were driving to Minneapolis earlier this summer and passed through Owatonna, MN the hometown of Adam Young of Owl City. I started thinking about what an actual city of owls would look like and the structure of the logic puzzle was born.
If you never made it here in the hunt but are reading notes (hello!), I'd encourage you to give this one a whirl. It's very fun and you can slack me if you get stuck. I have a full deductive chain written up in shorthand but I didn't feel like translating it into the solution notes. -JO
