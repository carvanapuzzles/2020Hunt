Each image is a depiction of an actor's last name. They are in alphabetical order and can be identied as follows:

Bale / Bang / Bridges / Butler

Clay / Ford / Garfield / Gibson

Gray / Hardy / Holland / Nero

Page / Pine / Shannon / Stamp

Stuart / Waltz / Wayne / West

The fact that there are 20 images and 10 sets of questionmarks might suggest pairing. Each of the sets can be filled in with the name of a character that two of these actors played at different times ("the same scripts play out over and over again, just with new faces"). The characters' names are in alphabetical order as well.

BATMAN (Christian Bale and Adam West)
DRACULA (Claes Bang and Gerard Butler)
ERNST BLOFELD (Charles Gray and Christoph Waltz)
GENERAL ZOD (Michael Shannon and Terrence Stamp)
JACK RYAN (Harrison Ford and Chris Pine)
KITTY PRYDE (Ellen Page and Katie Stuart)
LANCELOT (Nicholas Clay and Franco Nero)
MAX ROCKATANSKY (Mel Gibson and Tom Hardy)
ROOSTER COGBURN (Jeff Bridges and John Wayne)
SPIDER-MAN (Andrew Garfield and Tom Holland)

The highlighted letters spell out ACTOR POOTS. Solving the clue gives the answer IMOGEN.

Author's Notes
I think this puzzle is noticably harder than the others in round 1, and probably harder than a puzzle or two in round 2. By easing into rounds and ending with a bit of challenge, we hoped to enhance the sense of accomplishment upon completion of a round while still making them approachable and break-into-able with some easier puzzles up front.
As for the puzzle itself, it ended up being a lot harder to construct than I initially assumed when I thought of the idea. Keeping the character names in alphabetical order at the end was a real pain but necessary to avoid confusion around characters who might go by multiple aliases (do I enter Bruce Wayne or Batman?) or have the same number of letters in their name as another character. I spent a good while looking for an alternative to Kitty Pryde since Katie Stuart isn't very well-known but couldn't make it work. In the end though, I think it's still a nice puzzle and hopefully people enjoyed it! -JO
