The solver is presented with 6 numbered buttons that appear to consistently do the same thing when you press them (show the corresponding letter of I-N-S-A-N-E). The way to break into the puzzle is remembering the clued quote: "Insanity is doing the same thing over and over again and expecting different results."

Pressing a single button repeatedly for long enough will eventually give an unexpeced character with ~ on either side. Each number needs to be pressed a different amount of times to see this new character.

Button # of times pressed New result
1 83 ~A~
2 69 ~S~
3 84 ~C~
4 79 ~I~
5 70 ~I~
6 50 ~!~

As the letters suggest, use ASCII to translate the number of presses into characters. This yields SETOF2.

The process can be repeated on sets of buttons. Alternating 121212... eventually yields another unexpected character, as does every set of two. Ordering them by low-hi num and counting the number of cycles needed to give the unexpected letter gives:

Set # of times repeated New result
12 14 ~S~
13 15 ~O~
14 23 ~C~
15 20 ~R~
16 25 ~A~
23 16 ~Z~
24 5 ~Y~
25 9 ~I~
26 14 ~A~
34 16 ~M~
35 19 ~B~
36 25 ~L~
45 3 ~I~
46 8 ~N~
56 16 ~D~

Using 1=A, 2=B, etc, this time the number of repeats gives NOW TYPE IN PSYCHO. Taken with the shown letters, SO CRAZY I AM BLIND, the penultimate idea can be executed. Spelling out "Psycho" in braille on the pad! Pressing the numbers in ascending order per letter causes "~Hurley's Numbers~" to pop on the screen.

This refers to the sequence 4, 8, 15,16, 23, 42 from the TV show LOST. Pressing the first button 4 times, followed by the second button 8 times, etc, at last brings onto the screen "Your answer is the location of the art gallery known as "The Museum of Insanity" "

This inspirational establishment is located in EAU CLAIRE, Wisconsin.
