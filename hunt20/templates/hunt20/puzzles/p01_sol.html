Each of the clues can be answered in punny fashion with the name of a bone in the human body.

What type of verbal hesitation is serious?
STERNUM
Why did the worker stop driving to the office alone?
METACARPAL
What do you call a father who works at a bank?
PATELLA
How would one catch the doctor from Futurama?
TRAPEZOID
What do you call a lying cloud of space dust?
FIBULA
What sound does half of a frog make?
RIB
How do under-the-tongue thermometers work?
TEMPORAL
How do you take the natural logarithm of a?
ULNA
What did the farmer say after he fed his pigs?
HAMATE
What are most of these puns (hopefully)?
HUMERUS

The highlighted letters spell SET OF BONES, which clues the answer SKELETON.

Author's Notes
If you ask me what my favorite type of puzzle is, I will usually say something along the lines of "anything that makes me laugh." So I guess it's not a surprise that there's a dumb pun puzzle right up front.
My favorite joke that didn't make the cut--What did the man say when the green veggies were rolling off his plate? "Stapes!" This is really funny if you happen to know that this ear bone is pronounced "stay-peas" and really confusing if you've never heard of it and assume it rhymes with "capes" when you see it written out. All of our testsolvers fell into the latter category so now it lives here, in the author's notes. -JO
